Analysis

Наладится (Naladitsya) is a Russian pop song written by Russian music artist Eldar Dzharakhov. The song was released in December 2020 and contrasts the negative effects of the global pandemic with a positive, futuristic attitude.

The first lines of the song open with: 'The sky frowns, my hands are lowered; All this will be forgotten, and everything will be better'. Dzharakhov skillfully expresses the essence of the song in this one line - whatever circumstance/difficulty we come upon, we can still hope for a better future and do the best we can in the time that we have.

In addition to having meaning, the song also shows the typical characteristics of pandemic-style music. Lines like "So many people, but I'm all alone" and "The streets are deserted" clearly incorporate the theme of loneliness within the song. Dzharakhov however, in comparison to Sefo's grim declarations of "the law of the world" and "I won't tell her to come back" (Geri Gel), takes comfort in this theme. He sees a chance for introspection, that instead of listening to people's "empty talk" he can "find [himself] and find love".

Наладится expresses the fact that even in the worst of times, it's still possible to hope for better and do actions that help yourself and others around you. Dzharkhov regards introspection and discovering himself as these helpful actions. And indeed, the process of discovering oneself can often extricate people from the unhappiness of the every day world into the more authentic happiness of the soul.
